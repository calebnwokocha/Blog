Understanding Mutually Exclusive Events

What are Mutually Exclusive Events?

Imagine you are tossing a coin. It can land either heads or tails, but not both at the same time. These outcomes are mutually exclusive events—they cannot happen simultaneously. In real-life scenarios, mutually exclusive events occur more frequently than you might think.

  • In Business: A customer either purchases a product or doesn’t.
  • In Finance: The market either goes up or down in a given period.
  • In Daily Life: You either arrive on time or you’re late.

Our software is designed to handle these kinds of events, even when their probabilities are not known beforehand.
